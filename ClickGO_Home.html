ABOUT ART WOLFE

Art Wolfe's journey with photography began in the verdant landscapes of the Pacific Northwest. With a fine art background and a deep reverence for nature, he has traveled to every continent to capture the essence of our planet's wild places.

Over four decades, Wolfe has worked on every continent and in hundreds of locations, creating an unparalleled body of work. His photographs have appeared in National Geographic, Smithsonian, and Audubon magazines, and he has published over 100 books.

What sets Art apart is not just his technical mastery, but his ability to see and capture the intricate patterns, colors, and behaviors that make nature extraordinary. His background in the fine arts enables him to compose images that are not merely documentation, but works of art that inspire conservation and wonder.

From African wildlife to Antarctic icebergs, from mountain gorillas to migrating birds, Art's lens reveals the interconnectedness of all living things and the urgent need to protect Earth's remaining wild spaces.
